# Audit Controls

## What It Is

The Audit Controls service is your cross-service accounting governance layer - the system that manages approval policies, segregation-of-duties rules, signature requests, immutable audit events, and control exceptions across accounting workflows. It ensures finance automation remains accountable, reviewable, and defensible.

## Why Your Business Needs This

**The Problem**: As accounting workflows become automated, controls often remain scattered across roles, spreadsheets, email approvals, and application-specific logs. Teams can post, approve, pay, reverse, and modify financial records without one consistent control model or audit evidence trail.

**The Pain Points**:
- **Inconsistent Approvals**: Each service handles approvals differently
- **Segregation Risk**: The same user can create, approve, and execute sensitive transactions
- **Weak Evidence**: Audit logs exist, but they are not structured for control testing
- **Manual Sign-Offs**: Electronic signatures and certifications happen outside the workflow
- **Control Exceptions Hidden**: Policy violations are discovered after the fact
- **Cross-Service Blind Spots**: GL, AP, AR, tax, and consolidation controls are not evaluated together

## How It Delivers Pain Relief

### ✅ **Approval Policies**
Define reusable approval policies by service, action, amount, company, risk, role, and workflow state.

### 🚧 **Segregation-Of-Duties Rules**
Prevent high-risk combinations such as creating and approving the same payment, posting and reversing the same journal, or filing and approving the same tax return.

### ✍️ **Signature Requests**
Capture electronic signatures, certifications, and formal approvals for close, filings, payments, and sensitive accounting actions.

### 🔒 **Immutable Audit Events**
Store accounting audit events in a consistent stream so actions can be reconstructed, reviewed, and retained.

### ⚠️ **Control Exceptions**
Detect, assign, remediate, and evidence policy violations before they become audit findings.

### 🎯 **Business Impact**
- **Reduce Fraud And Error Risk**: Enforce approvals and segregation before sensitive actions complete
- **Improve Audit Readiness**: Keep structured evidence for control testing
- **Standardize Governance**: Apply one control model across accounting services
- **Support Enterprise Buyers**: Provide the GRC foundation expected by larger organizations

## Key Capabilities

- **Approval Policies**: Approval matrices, thresholds, service/action scope, and policy status
- **Segregation Rules**: SoD definitions, conflict detection, exemptions, and remediation requirements
- **Signature Requests**: Electronic sign-off, certification, status, signer, and evidence capture
- **Audit Events**: Immutable event stream for postings, approvals, reversals, filings, exports, and control decisions
- **Control Exceptions**: Exception detection, assignment, remediation, status, and audit evidence
- **Cross-Service Governance**: Control layer for GL, AP, AR, bank sync, tax, consolidation, revenue, lease, and reporting workflows
