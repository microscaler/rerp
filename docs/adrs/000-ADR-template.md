# ADR NNN: Decision title

- **Status**: PROPOSED
- **Date**: YYYY-MM-DD
- **Decision owners**: Team or accountable roles
- **Group**: Architecture area
- **Authority**: Normative
- **Scope**: stable.dot.separated.scope
- **Last reviewed**: YYYY-MM-DD
- **Supersedes**: None
- **Superseded by**: None

## Context

Describe the decision that is required, why it is required now, and the
business or engineering conditions that constrain it. Separate known facts
from assumptions.

## Decision

State the selected direction precisely. An accepted ADR should be clear enough
to distinguish a conforming implementation from a conflicting one.

## Assumptions

- List material assumptions that could invalidate or narrow the decision.

## Alternatives considered

### Alternative name

Explain the option, its benefits and costs, and why it was not selected.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Describe the intended benefits.

### Costs and risks

- Describe complexity, migration, operational, security, and compatibility
  consequences.

## Implementation and verification

Identify implementation anchors, acceptance tests, migration requirements, and
observable evidence. An ADR defines the decision; it is not proof that delivery
is complete.

## Related requirements

- Link the PRDs, modes of operation, and acceptance criteria driving this
  decision.

## Related decisions and artifacts

- Link related ADRs, OpenAPI contracts, service/suite READMEs, and external
  authorities with reviewed revisions where applicable.

## Notes

Record clarification and review notes that do not change the accepted outcome.
Material changes require a superseding ADR.
